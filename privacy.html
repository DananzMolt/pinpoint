Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026
The short version: Pinpoint runs entirely in your browser. It has no servers, collects no personal data, uses no analytics, and requires no account. Your comments and screenshots stay on your own device.

What Pinpoint does

Pinpoint is a browser extension for leaving comments on elements of a live web page and exporting them as a Markdown brief for an AI coding assistant. All of this happens locally, inside your browser.

Data we collect

None. Pinpoint does not send any data to us or to any third party. There is no backend, no telemetry, no tracking, no advertising identifiers, and no account system. We cannot see your pages, your comments, or your screenshots.

Data Pinpoint stores on your device

To work as expected, Pinpoint keeps a small amount of data in your browser's local storage, scoped to each website you use it on:

  • Your comments and their element references, so they persist across reloads of that site.
  • Whether Pinpoint is turned on for that site, plus your preferences (keyboard shortcuts, numbered pins, last-used markup color and thickness).

This data never leaves your device. You can clear it at any time from within the extension (Clear all) or by clearing your browser's site data.

Screenshots

When you capture an element screenshot, the image is created in your browser and saved to your own Downloads folder (or kept inline if saving is unavailable). Screenshots are never uploaded anywhere.

The hand-off brief

When you choose to hand off your comments, Pinpoint builds a Markdown summary and copies it to your clipboard. What you do with it next, for example pasting it into ChatGPT, Claude, Cursor, or Copilot, is your choice and is governed by that service's own privacy policy. Pinpoint itself sends nothing.

Permissions and why they are needed

  • activeTab: to capture a screenshot of the current tab when you ask for one.
  • downloads: to save a screenshot to your Downloads folder.
  • Access to the sites you visit (<all_urls>): so the widget can be turned on for any site. Pinpoint does not read or transmit page content unless you actively comment on or capture an element, and even then the result stays on your device.

Children

Pinpoint is a developer tool and is not directed at children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date. Because Pinpoint collects no data, changes will generally only clarify wording.
